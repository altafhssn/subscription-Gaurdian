Subscription Guardian is a Chrome extension that scans Gmail for subscription receipts and reveals the gap between what users think they spend vs. what they actually spend. Market data confirms a 2.5× perception gap — users estimate ~£68/mo while real spend averages ~£175/mo. This gap is the core growth engine:

"I thought I spent £65/mo on subscriptions. My Gmail says £180. Here's what I found." — post format that naturally generates viral Reddit threads

Product & Funnel

The Hook

Users connect Gmail via OAuth (read-only scope). The backend scans last 6 months of receipts and shows a surprise gap — "You think you spend £68/mo. You actually spend £175/mo. Here's where it's going." This revelation IS the marketing moment. Screenshots are inherently shareable.

Final Verdict

✅ BUILD — Bootstrapped micro-SaaS

Subscription Guardian is viable as a bootstrapped lifestyle business. The 2.5× perception gap is a genuine viral asset, costs are near-zero, and the UK market has a verified white space (no competitor combines Gmail scanning + Chrome extension + UK focus).

Key constraints:

  • £3.99/mo pricing makes paid acquisition uneconomical — stay organic
  • Google OAuth verification at 100 users is the biggest external bottleneck
  • Churn will be 6-8% without active retention work
  • This is a £500-2,000/mo MRR business, not a VC moonshot

Minimum viable next steps: Domain → Privacy policy → Railway deploy → Reddit post. Total cost: ~£54. Time to first user: 1 weekend.
