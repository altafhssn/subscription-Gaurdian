## 3. COST STRUCTURE

### 3.1 Gmail API Costs

Gmail API is **free** up to reasonable usage quotas:

| API | Daily Quota (free) | Per 1M Users Cost |
|-----|-------------------|------------------|
| `messages.list` | 1,000,000,000 units/day | Free (well under quota) |
| `messages.get` | 1,000,000,000 units/day | Free |
| `users.getProfile` | 1,000,000,000 units/day | Free |

**Key constraint:** Quota is shared across the entire Google Cloud project, not per user.
- Each user with 500 emails scanned: ~1,000 API calls (list + get per message)
- At 5,000 users scanning 500 emails each: 5M calls — well within daily limits
- **Verdict: Gmail API is effectively free at this scale**

**OAuth verification cost:**
- Free tier: <100 users (no verification needed — unlisted app)
- **Verification fee: £0** — Google doesn't charge for OAuth verification; it's a security review process
- Cost is in **time** (4-8 weeks) and **security audit expenses** ($15K-75K if using a consultancy, per Nylas data)
- Self-verification by solo founder: **£0-£500** (possible with thorough docs)
- Threshold: You need verification when Google flags sensitive/restricted scopes. Gmail read-only **will** require verification eventually (≥100 users). Budget **£200-£500** for legal review of security documentation.

### 3.4 Stripe Processing Fees (UK Domestic Cards)

- **Transaction fee:** 1.5% + 20p per charge (Stripe UK standard)
- **£3.99 payment:** 1.5% × £3.99 = £0.06 + £0.20 = **£0.26 per transaction**
- **Effective rate:** 6.5% of transaction value
- At 100 premium users: ~£26/mo in Stripe fees
- At 500 premium users: ~£130/mo in fees

**Alternative:** GoCardless (Direct Debit) — 1% + £0.20, capped at £2. Could be better for UK users.

## 4. UNIT ECONOMICS

### 4.1 Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Organic (Year 1 — no paid spend):**
- CAC = £0 direct cost
- But **opportunity cost** (founder time on content/SEO): ~20-40 hrs/mo
- At £50/hr founder time: implicit CAC of ~£5-15 per premium user
- **Effective organic CAC (implicit): ~£12 per premium user**

**Paid (when scaling):**
- Google Ads PPC ("forgotten subscriptions UK", "track subscriptions UK"): CPC £1.50-3.50
- Landing page → free signup: 13.5% → premium: 5.2%
- Cost per free signup: ~£11-26
- **Cost per premium user (paid): ~£220-500**

### 4.2 Lifetime Value (LTV)

**Assumptions:**
- Monthly churn (fintech avg): 5-8%/mo  
- Free → premium conversion: 5.2%
- Monthly subscription: £3.99

| Churn Rate | Avg Lifetime (months) | LTV (monthly only) | LTV (with 15% annual upgrades) |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------|-------------------------------|
| 5%/mo (best case, SaaS) | 20 | £79.80 | ~£88 |
| 6.5%/mo (fintech avg) | 15.4 | £61.45 | ~£68 |
| 8%/mo (consumer app avg) | 12.5 | £49.88 | ~£55 |

**Conservative LTV: ~£55**  
**Expected LTV: ~£68**  
**Best Case LTV: ~£88**

### 4.3 Months to Breakeven per Customer

With £0 direct acquisition cost (organic):
- **Breakeven: immediate** (first month's £3.99 > £0 variable cost)
- Variable cost per premium user: ~£0.26 (Stripe fee)
- Gross margin per user: £3.73/mo (93.5%)
- **Months to recover fixed infrastructure:**
  - 100 users: ~6 months (£23 fixed / £3.73 per user)
  - 500 users: ~2 months
  - 1,000 users: ~1.5 months

### 4.4 LTV:CAC Ratio

| Scenario | LTV | CAC | LTV:CAC | Verdict |
|----------|-----|-----|---------|---------|
| Organic (implicit) | £68 | £12 | **5.7:1** | ✅ Excellent |
| Organic (explicit £0) | £68 | £0 | **∞:1** | ✅ Infinite (not sustainable at scale) |
| Paid channels (PPC) | £68 | £220-500 | **0.14-0.31:1** | ❌ Unprofitable — don't do PPC yet |
| Paid (optimised, £150 CPA) | £68 | £150 | **0.45:1** | ❌ Still unprofitable |
| After optimisation (LTV £88, CPA £75) | £88 | £75 | **1.17:1** | ⚠️ Marginal (target ≥3:1) |

**Key Insight:** At £3.99/mo, paid acquisition is nearly impossible to make work in the UK. You need the LTV to hit £150+ (CPA of £50). This means either:
- Raise price to £6.99-9.99/mo
- Build viral loops (viral coefficient >1)
- Annual billing (improves retention)
- Or stay organic-only until scaling is proven
