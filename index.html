Trust

Read-only Gmail

Google's official OAuth, scoped to receipts. We can't send mail, can't delete, can't read drafts. You can revoke from your Google account at any time.

Your Gmail tokens are encrypted with PBKDF2-derived keys before they ever touch our database. Even our team can't read them in plaintext.

Open-source backend

Questions

Things people ask before signing up.

Can't see yours? Email hello@subscriptionguardian.co.uk — we read everything.

What does "read-only Gmail" actually mean?

We use Google's official OAuth flow with the gmail.readonly scope. Google itself enforces that we can't send, modify, or delete anything. We only read message subjects, senders, and snippets — and only the ones that look like receipts. You can revoke access from your Google account settings in a couple of clicks.

Do you actually cancel subscriptions for me?

Honest answer: no. We surface them and link you straight to each provider's cancellation page so you can do it in two clicks. Nobody can legally cancel a subscription on your behalf without your card details, and we don't ask for those. We're a magnifying glass, not a middleman.

Will it work with UK-specific services?

Yes. We've tuned the scanner for UK senders — Now TV, BT, Sky, PureGym, Tesco Mobile, Octopus, Vodafone, Three, and so on. If a service emails you a £-denominated receipt, we'll find it. If we miss one, tell us and we'll add it the same week.

What happens to my data when I leave?

Click Delete account in settings. Everything we hold — Gmail tokens, detected subscriptions, scan history — is wiped within 24 hours. We don't keep "shadow" backups for marketing purposes. You're entitled to a copy under UK GDPR; just email us and we'll send it within 30 days.

Why do I need an account at all?

You don't really. Scanning runs every time you log in, results sit in our database so we can show them to you. If you'd rather not have an account, just sign in, take a screenshot of your subscriptions, and delete the account. Free, takes five minutes.

Is this really free? What's the catch?

The free tier is genuinely free — capped at 10 subscriptions and a one-off scan because that covers our infrastructure costs. Guardian (£3/month) is for people who want to keep monitoring. We don't sell your data. We don't run ads. Either you pay us a few pounds a month or you don't — that's it.
